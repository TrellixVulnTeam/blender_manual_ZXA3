I was born into a Chinese ethnic family in Hanoi Vietnam. My grandparents were classified as capitalists and had worked for the French colonial government in Hanoi. My family encountered great difficulties because all their properties were confiscated and were put under house arrest for many years. The situation got worse after the 1979 Vietnam-China border war, causing many of the family members to leave Vietnam. Although I grew up after this period, I also suffered great stigma from communist regime. I could not find a way to develop myself to a successful employee after graduated from the Surveying and Land/Environmental Management degree course. I was advised by my father that I should leave Vietnam for England, which has the most democracy and respect for human rights in the world. Coming to England I had worked at multiple jobs and life turned out to be rather frustratingly difficult. I later met my wife, and we now have 4 sons (two from ours and two from her previous relationship). We have a nail salon and are both working there. Our life is currently stable and settled.
