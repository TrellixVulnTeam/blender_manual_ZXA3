Get a few turnips, and some carrots and use this (https://www.amazon.co.uk/OXO-Good-Grips-Swivel-Peeler/dp/B00004OCIP/ref=sr_1_1_sspa?dchild=1&keywords=peeler&qid=1601560005&sr=8-1-spons&psc=1&spLa=ZW5jcnlwdGVkUXVhbGlmaWVyPUEyUlFPSEMzNEVXN0tMJmVuY3J5cHRlZElkPUEwMjg0MTc4MVo5V0NKNEgzSllJMyZlbmNyeXB0ZWRBZElkPUEwOTU3MDE1MVdEN0U5OVAxNDEwSCZ3aWRnZXROYW1lPXNwX2F0ZiZhY3Rpb249Y2xpY2tSZWRpcmVjdCZkb05vdExvZ0NsaWNrPXRydWU=) to peel their skin off, use this (https://www.amazon.co.uk/Stainless-Chopper-Kitchen-Vegetable-Gadgets/dp/B07PRGCFV9/ref=psdc_3187149031_t2_B07JVCW68L) to hold the fruits flat and firm on a chopping board, then use this (https://www.amazon.co.uk/Lakeland-Sharp-Peel-Julienne-Peeler/dp/B00FGOUVRC/ref=sr_1_10?crid=28EUA1LIFA3CA&dchild=1&keywords=julienne+peeler&qid=1601560128&s=kitchen&sprefix=juli%2Ckitchen%2C145&sr=1-10) to shred the roots into thin strands. Soak them in boiling water and keep them there until cool. Lift them out of the water and put in another cooking pot. Make a juice (suggar, vinegar, maggi sauce, and some chilli sauce, and chilli slices if your have some fresh laying around) and soak the shredded vegs in. Leave overnight. You can have it with anything, from sandwiches, to cooked noodles and rice. Very tasty they are.
